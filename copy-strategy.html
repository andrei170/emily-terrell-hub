Foundation

Copy Strategy - Emily Terrell

Foundation doc · Voice, brand pillars, headline bank, framing AG1 will write to.

Voice

Warm, direct, peer-to-peer. Emily is a working RE coach and AI strategist who talks to other agents the way a colleague does - knowledgeable but approachable, specific but not clinical. The voice treats the reader as a professional who knows her craft but wants a smarter way to run her business. Not guru. Not motivational. Not corporate. Direct, collegial, and grounded in real RE workflow.

Use: agents, professionals, system, workflow, implement, practical, earn, build, run, tested, specific, step-by-step, straightforward, no-fluff Avoid: unlock, game-changer, revolutionary, transform your business (without a specific number attached), hustle, abundance, mindset shift (unless paired with a concrete action), AI magic (it is a tool, not a spell)

Brand Pillars

  1. Practical AI adoption - Emily teaches real estate agents how to use AI as a practical business tool, not a trend. Every deliverable should reflect that AI is a skill set to build, not a shortcut to admire from a distance.
  2. Business systems for RE professionals - the coaching is about building a business that runs on systems, not on the agent being present for every step. AI is one layer of that.
  3. Speaker authority - Emily holds stage credibility in the AI-for-RE space. Copy should reflect this without overstating it. "Top AI-for-RE speaker" is the wedge, not the whole identity.
  4. Peer-to-peer register - Emily speaks to agents as a fellow professional who has figured out something worth sharing. No authority-from-above framing. No "I will teach you" condescension. Collegial throughout.

Headline Bank

For the AI Realtor System landing page, hub callouts, and ad creative:

  • The system behind the AI talk. For agents ready to implement it.
  • AI for real estate, built for the agent who wants it to actually work.
  • The AI Realtor System. Less than 7 days to install. Built from what works on real transactions.
  • Five modules. Built by an AI-for-RE speaker. For agents at any tech comfort level.
  • The course for agents who are done watching tutorials and want a system that runs.
  • From conference talk to full implementation. The missing step.
  • You have heard the AI talk. This is what you do on Monday.
  • Listings, leads, follow-up, CMA, market reports. AI handles all five. Here is how.

Framing for the Funnel

  • Top of funnel (scorecard, IG hooks, blog CTAs): workflow-pain led, not vanity led. "Your follow-up is still manual, your listing descriptions still take 40 minutes, your CMA still starts from scratch" - specific workflow friction beats inspiration.
  • Mid funnel (course landing, scorecard results): AI Readiness Scorecard as the bridge. The score reveals the gap; the AI Realtor System closes it.
  • High funnel (1:1 coaching path): professional-to-professional framing. "If the system is in place and you want to scale beyond it, this is the conversation."

Banned Phrases

Universal AG1 ban list, plus Emily-specific:

  • No "unlock your potential" or any unlock framing
  • No "revolutionise your real estate business"
  • No "transform" unless paired with a specific metric (transaction volume, hours saved, follow-up rate)
  • No "cutting-edge AI" - dated, vague, over-used in RE marketing
  • No "AI-powered everything" as a generic claim
  • No first-month-lift language without a real client result to back it

Tone Calibration By Surface

Surface Register Length
IG hook Specific to a workflow pain or a counterintuitive claim 1 line
Email subject Direct question or specific claim, no clickbait 5-9 words
VSL opening "Here is what I found after testing AI inside a real estate workflow for two years." 1 sentence cold open
Landing hero What the AI Realtor System delivers + who it is for 1 promise + 1 mechanism
Scorecard result copy Personalised, specific to score tier, peer-to-peer 2-3 paragraphs
FAQ Plain answers, no hedge, no corporate softness 2-3 sentences each

Voice Test

Every paragraph passes if a working RE agent reads it and recognises a problem from her own week. If it could appear in a generic "scale your business" coaching ad, rewrite it.
